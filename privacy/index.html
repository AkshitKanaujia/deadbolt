Deadbolt Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2, 2026

The short version: Deadbolt collects no data. There is no account, no server, and no tracking.

What Deadbolt does with your information

Deadbolt is an app blocker built on Apple's Screen Time (Family Controls) framework. Everything the app works with stays on your iPhone:

  • Your app selections. The apps and categories you choose to block are handled by Apple's framework as opaque, privacy-preserving tokens. Deadbolt cannot see the names of the apps you select, and this information never leaves your device.
  • Your sessions and stats. Session history, schedules, and the "hours reclaimed" counter are stored locally on your device and are not transmitted anywhere.
  • Your NFC tag. If you register an NFC tag as a physical key, its hardware identifier is stored only on your device.

What we collect

Nothing. Deadbolt contains no analytics, no advertising SDKs, and makes no network requests of its own. We have no servers and no database. We could not read your data even if we wanted to — it never reaches us.

Purchases

Subscriptions are processed entirely by Apple through your App Store account. We do not receive your name, email address, or payment details. See Apple's Privacy Policy for how Apple handles purchase data.

Children

Deadbolt is designed for adults managing their own screen time and is not directed at children under 13.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted at this address with a new "last updated" date. Because the app collects nothing, meaningful changes are unlikely.
